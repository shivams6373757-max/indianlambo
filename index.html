The Great Lamborghini

Lamborghini, the iconic Italian sports car manufacturer, was founded in 1963 by Ferruccio Lamborghini, a successful tractor manufacturer.
Driven by a desire to create a better sports car than Ferrari, he established the company in Sant'Agata Bolognese.
The first model, the 350 GTV, debuted at the Turin Motor Show in 1963, followed by the 350 GT, the first car for sale, in 1964.
Lamborghini quickly gained recognition for its powerful, refined, and uniquely designed vehicles, including the legendary Miura.
Despite facing financial challenges and ownership changes, including a period under Chrysler's ownership, Lamborghini has remained a symbol of
automotive excellence and continues to produce high-performance supercars.
Today, Lamborghini is part of the Volkswagen Group, maintaining its reputation for innovation and pushing the boundaries of automotive engineering.
